Raven: The Asylum

Raven: The Asylum was a Unity C# game made focusing on horror elements, and inclusion of AI.


A game made by Niven Dias for the Individual Project 2023 University of Sussex.

Features and About

The Task

Raven: The Asylum was a game made focusing on horror elements, and inclusion of AI, as part of my Dissertation, in which I scored a First Class with a 75% mark from the University of Sussex.

I worked throughout my third year to both write my dissertation, create plans of it, and to code the game itself.

Features

When working on this project, there were many features created to create the kind of game I wanted to create.

-AI systems in the form of a node based coded behaviour tree in C#

-Flocking Mechanics of boids

-Third person and gameplay design

-Level design

-Spatial audio and distance recognition

-Horror elements, and more.

Inspirations

There were three key inspirations for this game, of which 2 were identified during a questionaire without being prompted for the response.

These three games were

Alien: Isolation - A key inspiration, and was confirmed as the core inspiration for the game after reaching out to a member of the Lead programming team for the game at Creative Assembly.

Slender: The Eight Pages - Formed the majority of the ambient feel of the game's inspiration, specifically the design of the levels and the decisions made about player visibility in the world.

Little Nightmares - Made inspiration as to the gameplay feel of the player - Wanting them to feel trapped in a maze like environment with the monster.
